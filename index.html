Proactive Voice Privacy Protection for Chinese Zero-Shot Voice Cloning with Perceptually Constrained Perturbations
Yiwei Hu, Kuntharrgyal Khysru, Quecuo De, Ethan Deci, Di Jin, and Jianguo Wei
Abstract. Recent zero-shot text-to-speech (TTS) and voice conversion (VC) models can clone a target speaker's voice from only a short reference utterance, raising concerns about unauthorized voice misuse and voice privacy leakage. This paper studies proactive voice privacy protection for Chinese zero-shot voice cloning, where reference speech is protected before it is exposed to potential misuse. We propose PsyVo, a proactive protection method operating in the short-time Fourier transform (STFT) magnitude domain. PsyVo combines directional centroid guidance with dynamic psychoacoustic masking projection: the former guides the protected speech embedding away from the source speaker and toward a non-target identity region, while the latter constrains the perturbation distribution according to the auditory masking structure of the reference speech. To evaluate the method, we construct a Chinese zero-shot cloning evaluation setting with high-risk Chinese synthesis texts and conduct experiments under both TTS-based and VC-based cloning scenarios. Experimental results based on automatic speaker verification (ASV) show that PsyVo achieves effective defense performance, better preserves the perceptual quality of protected reference speech than conventional perturbation baselines, and maintains the usability of generated speech.
Demo description. This page provides representative audio examples for one female speaker and one male speaker. For each speaker, we show the original reference speech, protected reference speech generated by different methods, and cloned speech generated by XTTS-v2 or OpenVoice-v2. In the OpenVoice-v2 setting, the file beginning with risk_ is the source speech used for voice conversion. The synthesis text is aligned with the corresponding Chinese risk text used in the paper.
